A kickstarter delivery this week - the [Cipherling](https://cipherling.com) by [Mother Ultimate (campaign)](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/motherultimate/make-100-cipherling-a-numbers-station-robot/description). A lovely little small batch machine (part of the [Make100](https://www.kickstarter.com/make-100) series) that listens for [numbers station broadcasts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wvr6o7fBcTY) (actually connects via wifi to their remote receiver) and when a transmission is found, Cipherling alerts with a lovely audio ping and then you can listen to the broadcast, just like the one below (turn audio on).

Almost the end of term and had a great guest lecture this week. Joshua came to talk about his work at [Hildebrand technology](https://www.hildebrand.co.uk) and in particular his experience of moving production of their IHD product ([Glow](https://www.hildebrand.co.uk/products/ihd-cad)) from China back to the UK. Some really interesting insights into their design process and was great to see videos of his experience of a week working on the assembly line in Shenzen to understand how his product was being made. Was also taken by the observation that only one of our students had ever been in a factory. I would love to introduce a site visit to an electonics assembly facility as part of the course.
